Trade-Offs in AI-Text Detection: A Comparative Study of DistilBERT, ALBERT, and TinyBERT

👥   Authors : Ajjay Adhithya V, Garav Malik, Dr. Sahaya Beni Prathiba, Dr. Dhanalakshmi Ranganayakulu

📝   Yet to be Published in International Conference on Sustainable Communication Networks and Application ICSCN 2025

📖   Publisher : IEEE ( Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers )


VIEW PUBLICATION        VIEW GITHUB    

Note: Publication link may not work as the paper is yet to be published

♦ Natural Language Processing (NLP), Machine Learning (Transformer Models)

This paper presents a comparative study of three lightweight transformer models: DistilBERT, ALBERT, and TinyBERT for the task of detecting AI-generated text. It evaluates the trade-offs between classification accuracy and computational efficiency, such as inference time and model size, against a traditional TF-IDF and Logistic Regression baseline. The study concludes that TinyBERT offers the most practical balance, achieving a high F1-Score of 0.9936 while being significantly faster and smaller than the other models. This makes it an ideal candidate for scalable, real-world deployment where resources may be limited.

LoRaWAN-Based Blood Sugar Monitoring to Enhance Diabetes Management in Rural Areas

👥   Author : Ajjay Adhithya V

📖   Publisher : Zenodo (Open-access repository)

🔑   DOI : 10.5281/zenodo.17309300

🗓   10 October 2025


VIEW PUBLICATION

♦ Internet of Things (IoT), Embedded Systems, Machine Learning

This paper proposes a conceptual framework for a LoRaWAN-based blood glucose monitoring system aimed at improving diabetes management in rural areas with limited healthcare access. Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) sensors transmit patient data over a long-range, low-power network to a centralized server, enabling remote monitoring by healthcare professionals without relying on internet connectivity. The framework also envisions the use of machine learning algorithms to analyze historical glucose data, predict trends, and generate automated alerts for abnormal glucose levels. Together, these features offer a scalable and cost-effective approach to enhance timely diabetes care in low-resource settings.
